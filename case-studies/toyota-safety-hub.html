Toyota Safety Hub

toyota.com/safety-sense

I designed Toyota Safety Hub, a dual-mode destination that unified Toyota's fragmented safety content for shoppers and owners — growing monthly views from 18K to 50K+, a 178% lift.

Role

Product Designer

Client

Toyota

Agency

Saatchi & Saatchi LA

Shipped

July 2023

Context

Toyota's safety technology is industry-leading — spanning Toyota Safety Sense, Toyota For Families, and award-winning crash test performance. But when users actually arrived on toyota.com to learn about it, the story fell apart. Safety content was scattered across disconnected pages, each touting different features in different ways, and no single page could answer the questions either shoppers or owners arrived with.

1

Safety content was scattered

Commitments to safety lived across multiple areas of the site, each surfacing different features with no unifying narrative.

2

No depth for shoppers

Toyota shoppers researching a purchase couldn't find the educational content needed to make an informed decision.

3

No specificity for owners

Owners had no way to discover which safety features were actually equipped on their vehicle, or how to use them.

A study of 221 Toyota.com visitors confirmed what the audit surfaced:

74%

Couldn't fully accomplish their goal during their visit.

48%

Rated the existing experience "very difficult."

43%

Said the whole thing took too much time.

Problem

The safety section wasn't just underperforming — it was actively failing the people it was supposed to serve. As I dug deeper, the reason became clear: the existing experience had been built for no one in particular.

Two completely different users were showing up with completely different goals. Toyota shoppers were researching a future purchase — arriving logged out, looking for clarity and credibility on how Toyota's safety technology compared. Toyota owners already had a Toyota in their garage and wanted specific answers about the features on their specific vehicle. Serving both without compromising either became the central design challenge.

Pain points
Safety content scattered across disconnected pages with no unifying story.
Shoppers couldn't find the depth needed to evaluate Toyota confidently.
Owners couldn't see which safety features were equipped on their car.
74% of visitors left without accomplishing what they came to do.
Needs
A single destination that consolidates every safety story in one place.
Clarity and credibility for shoppers evaluating a Toyota purchase.
Vehicle-specific answers for owners who already drive a Toyota.
An experience that actually helps users finish the task they arrived with.

Solution

I ran a competitive audit across automotive peers and out-of-vertical leaders. Subaru struck an accessible, first-time-buyer tone, bringing information-heavy pages to life with interactive components and testimonials that made complexity feel approachable. Volvo treated safety as a core brand principle, building trust through a show-don't-tell narrative spread across more safety subpages than any other brand I examined. Neither was the exact answer, but together they pointed at what Toyota was missing: an experience that was both functionally specific and emotionally resonant.

I developed three concepts, each representing a different strategic bet — and let the research pick the winner.

Concept 1

Simple

A utilitarian deep-dive into each TSS system. Clean and functional for owners, but too narrow for shoppers still making a purchase decision.

Concept 2 — selected

Safety Hub

A dual-mode experience: structured overview with opt-in depth for shoppers, vehicle-specific personalization for owners.

Concept 3

Safety Overview

Editorial and brand-forward. Engaging for browsing, but too thin on the specifics owners actually needed.

The research made the decision clear. Concept 1 left shoppers without a story; Concept 3 left owners without answers. Concept 2, Safety Hub, was the only direction that served both user types without compromise — and I advocated for it on those grounds.

The shipped experience split by login state. Shoppers got a logged-out destination with a structured overview of Toyota Safety Sense, interactive components for exploration, and an opt-in depth model that respected their time — enough context to make a confident decision without forcing all of it at once. Owners got a logged-in experience personalized to their vehicle, leading with the TSS system equipped on their car and surfacing content relevant to their model. For older vehicles without a TSS system, the page fell back to an agnostic view of standard safety features, so no owner ever arrived at a dead end.

Shopper

Logged-out experience

Structured TSS overview, interactive components for exploration, and an opt-in depth model that respects the user's time.

Owner

Logged-in experience

Personalized to the vehicle in the driveway — leading with the equipped TSS system, with an agnostic fallback for older models.

Outcome

Toyota Safety Hub launched in July 2023 and became the primary safety destination on toyota.com, growing monthly views from 18K to over 50K — a 178% lift. The win was definitional as much as quantitative. By building around two clearly defined user types rather than "everyone," the redesign finally gave shoppers and owners what the old experience never had: a single place on toyota.com that actually answered their questions.

50K+

Monthly page views on the new Safety Hub — up from 18K before the redesign.

+178%

Lift in traffic to Toyota's primary safety destination.
