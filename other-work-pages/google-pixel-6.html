Pixel 6 Launch Sandbox

New York City

I led UX and creative-directed nine in-store animations for Pixel 6's launch inside Google Store NYC — turning the phone's most advanced camera features into a walk-through gallery that opened on launch day.

Role

Lead UX Designer
Animator

Client

Google

Agency

Deeplocal

Shipped

October 2021

Context

Google Store's Sandbox Rooms are immersive enclosures built to make flagship hardware tangible — guests step inside, and the product speaks for itself. For Pixel 6's launch, Google and Deeplocal needed one room to carry three of the phone's newest camera capabilities at once.

1

Motion Mode

Long-exposure motion captured computationally — a feature that lives in movement, not stills.

2

Magic Eraser

A post-capture edit that only makes sense when you see the before and the after.

3

Super Res Zoom

20× computational detail — impossible to perceive at thumbnail scale, hard to convey on a wall.

Problem

Great cameras sell themselves through the photos they take — but a physical room can't hand every guest a phone and hope for the best. We had to translate Pixel 6's most sophisticated features into something a guest could read, understand, and feel in the seconds it takes to walk through a room.

Challenge
Static prints can't demonstrate motion or edit-based features.
Retail dwell time is measured in seconds, not minutes.
Nine separate animations had to feel like one cohesive gallery.
A tight launch-day deadline with distributed freelance animators.
Approach
Use wipes and fades to reveal before-and-after states.
Lead with a single striking image per feature, not captions.
Hold one visual language — timing, easing, type — across every screen.
Daily working sessions, biweekly reviews, on-site QA through launch.

Solution

We staged the room as a gallery. Each wall became a large-format print, and each print came to life with a single, patient animation — usually a before-and-after reveal — that demonstrated the feature without crowding the image. Photography did the selling; motion did the explaining.

As the only creative leading the animators, I started the process as if I'd be animating every screen myself — sourcing reference, writing frame-by-frame direction, and setting the timing standard before any work went into production. Daily working sessions and biweekly creative reviews kept nine separate hands feeling like one.

The final room paired nine on-screen animations with three digital plaques — anchoring the camera features alongside the For All You Are campaign and a quote wall from photographer Kennedi Carter, whose Real Tone work grounded the whole gallery.

Outcome

The Sandbox Room opened on Pixel 6's launch day inside Google Store NYC. Guests who walked in to buy the phone walked through the gallery first — turning a purchase moment into a reason to linger.
